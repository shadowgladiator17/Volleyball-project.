Detailed History of the Sport

Volleyball was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan in Holyoke, Massachusetts. Originally called "Mintonette," it was created as an indoor sport for businessmen who found basketball too vigorous. In 1896, the name was changed to "Volleyball" after the first public exhibition at Springfield College. The first custom-made ball was manufactured by Spalding in 1900. Since then, the ball has evolved from a three-layer leather design to the heat-bonded, dimpled synthetic shells used in the 2026 Olympic cycles.
